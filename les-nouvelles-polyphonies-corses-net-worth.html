An Evolving Tradition of Corsican Music

Les Nouvelles Polyphonies Corses burst onto the music scene in 1991, showcasing a rich blend of traditional Corsican sounds and modern musical influences.

The group, founded by Patrizia Poli and Patrizia Gattaceca, emerged from the Corsican Roots Revival movement of the 1970s, aiming to breathe new life into the indigenous music of Corsica.

This movement was pivotal, as it aimed to preserve the island’s cultural heritage while making it relevant for contemporary audiences.

The Birth of a Unique Sound

Their debut album, produced by Hector Zazou, is a cornerstone of their legacy, featuring a mix of local and international musicians, including Manu Dibango on saxophone and Jon Hassell on trumpet.

These collaborations underscored the group’s commitment to the evolving concept of polyphony, integrating instruments like the tabla, djembe, and ghatam to create a global sound palette rooted in Corsican traditions.

A Global Stage

Their performance at the opening ceremony of the 1992 Winter Olympics in Albertville, France, catapulted them onto the world stage, offering a vast audience a taste of Corsican polyphony.

This appearance, coupled with the subsequent use of their song Giramondu in a global Philips advertisement, significantly raised their international profile, blending traditional Corsican music with modern influences and electronic music arranged by Zazou.

Recognition and Reverberation

The album’s success, marked by over 100,000 copies sold in France alone and an award for the best album in the traditional music category at the 1992 Victoires de la Musique, reflects their impact.
